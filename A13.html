Who Is It?

"Tung Tung Sahur" is a rhythmic phrase commonly heard during Ramadan in parts of South and Southeast Asia. Traditionally used to wake people for the pre-dawn meal (sahur), the chant has become a viral sensation online.

Origins of the Phrase

The phrase mimics the beat of drums ("Tung Tung") used by local volunteers who walk the streets to wake up neighbors for sahur. It’s both practical and deeply cultural.

Going Viral

Thanks to platforms like TikTok and Instagram, videos featuring animated shouts of "Tung Tung Sahur!" gained traction globally, often remixed for comedy or parody.

Symbolism vs. Satire

While now often used for laughs, the phrase still carries cultural weight. It highlights communal life, religious tradition, and the merging of ancient customs with internet humor.

Did You Know?

In some towns, sahur drummers are sponsored with treats or tips from locals – and in some cases, they’ve become minor celebrities!
